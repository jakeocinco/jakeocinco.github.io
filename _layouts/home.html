Work Experience

Professionally, I had many amazing work opportunities during my tenure as a college student, spending time at both L3Harris and 84.51. I did my first two semesters of work at L3Harris (Fall 2018, Summer 2019), where I was thrust into a work environment for the first time. I was involved in a large-scale software development project, where I played a part developing software to interface with visible and infrared cameras. Most importantly, I learned how to operate amongst and lead a team, while undergoing the rigors of testing and documentation involving large releases.

My second co-op was at 84.51 (Spring and Summer 2020), where my work environment and responsibilities shifted dramatically. I worked as a full stack developer, primarily writing functionality and unit tests for a Java Spring Boot backend and Angular frontend. I was also able to explore and contribute to the management of our docker containers and databases, two technologies I had yet to encounter. Due to the schedule of my classes, I was able to spend an extended amount of time on my co-op experience at 84.51, working for eight consecutive months, which allowed me to really embed myself into the team. On an unlucky note, the COVID-19 pandemic caused a good portion of this time to be spent working from home, which forced me to quickly adapt to a very new environment once again.

I returned to 84.51 the following Summer (2021). This time in a different role, I am working as a data engineer on a team shifting to a more cloud focused data delivery solution. This role is requiring me to quickly upskill in the development of azure pipelines and database management.
